Dear Editors of The Ramanujan Journal,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript

  "Differential transcendence of the Hahn-Exton q-cosine and the arithmetic of its zeros."

Summary. The paper proves that the Hahn-Exton (third Jackson) q-cosine
G(q,z) = 1phi1(0;q;q^2,z) = sum_k (-1)^k q^{k(k-1)} z^k / (q;q)_{2k}
is differentially transcendental over C(z) for every 0 < q < 1: its second-order q-difference
equation has difference Galois group containing SL_2 and admits no sigma-delta integrability
relation, so the parametrized difference Galois theory of Hardouin-Singer and Arreche-Dreyfus-
Roques applies. Complementing the function-level result, the paper evaluates the regularized
product of the positive zeros exactly,
  prod_{k>=1} z_k(q) q^{2(k-1)} = (q;q^2)_infty,
and develops the integral arithmetic of the zero lattice: every rescaled zero is an integer power
series (Newton-polygon + Hensel), the deviations switch on exactly at the triangular numbers
(hexagonal law for the cosine side, its counterpart for the q-sine), and both interleaved lattices'
deviations stabilize q-adically to a single eta-quotient. These results frame one concrete open
question -- transcendence of the zeros z_k(q) at algebraic q -- which specializes to a planar
reflection-group growth constant beta_2 (OEIS A396406), for which the paper proves an unconditional
effective non-rationality bound.

Fit. The work lies in the journal's core areas: q-series, basic hypergeometric / q-special
functions, and their arithmetic. All computational statements are backed by openly archived code and
interval-arithmetic certification data (Zenodo concept DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20370090).

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has not been published
previously. I am an independent researcher with no competing interests.
