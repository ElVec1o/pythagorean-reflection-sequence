----- NAME -----
Growth function of the reflection group of a three-dimensional orthoscheme with two equal edge lengths and one distinct (Class B), for edge lengths (1, 1, 2).

----- DATA -----
1, 4, 9, 18, 35, 67, 129, 249, 480, 925, 1783, 3437, 6625, 12770, 24591, 47298, 90948, 174939, 336544

----- OFFSET -----
0,2

----- COMMENTS -----
The three-dimensional orthoscheme (path simplex) is a tetrahedron with four vertices V_0, V_1, V_2, V_3 whose consecutive edges V_0V_1, V_1V_2, V_2V_3 are mutually perpendicular; here the edge lengths are (1, 1, 2), Class B (two equal, one distinct). W is the reflection group generated by the reflections in the four bounding faces, and a(n) is the number of distinct group elements of word-length n.

Unlike the distinct-length (Class C) case, with two equal edges the geometric realization is not faithful, so a(n) is not known to equal the growth series of any fixed Coxeter group, and no closed form or rational generating function is known. The growth is exponential with empirical ratio near 1.925 (a(18)/a(17) = 1.9238); no linear recurrence over Q of order <= 9 fits the data.

Values computed by exact-rational breadth-first search on edge lengths (1, 1, 2) through word-length 18, triple-verified.

Leg-independence within Class B is observed empirically, not proved: the same initial terms arise for edge lengths (1,1,2), (1,1,3), (2,2,3), (1,1,5), and (3,3,7). Leg-independence is proved only for Class C (pairwise distinct edges); that proof does not transfer, since it relies on all dihedral angles being irrational multiples of Pi, which fails when two edges are equal.

The tetranacci recurrence t(n) = t(n-1) + t(n-2) + t(n-3) + t(n-4) (cf. A251704) reproduces a(6) through a(13) but diverges at n = 14, predicting 24615 versus the true a(14) = 24591; its dominant root 1.92756... is a coincidence, not the growth rate.

----- FORMULA -----
(No closed form or rational generating function is known.)

----- EXAMPLE -----
a(1) = 4: the four reflections in the bounding faces are the four elements of word-length 1.
