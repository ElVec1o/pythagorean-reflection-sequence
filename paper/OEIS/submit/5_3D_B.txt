----- NAME -----
BFS layer sizes of the 3D right-corner orthoscheme reflection group with two equal legs and one distinct leg (Class B), computed for legs (1,1,2).

----- DATA -----
1, 4, 9, 18, 35, 67, 129, 249, 480, 925, 1783, 3437, 6625, 12770, 24591, 47298, 90948, 174939, 336544

----- OFFSET -----
0,2

----- COMMENTS -----
The 3D right-corner orthoscheme has three mutually perpendicular legs at a vertex V_0. W = <R_0, R_1, R_2, R_3> is generated by the reflections across the four faces; a(n) is the number of distinct group elements of minimal W-word-length n (the size of BFS layer n in the Cayley graph).

Class B denotes the case of exactly two equal legs and one distinct leg. No closed form is known; the sequence appears to exhibit intermediate (non-polynomial, sub-exponential-rate) growth and is not produced by any short linear recurrence.

The associated Coxeter diagram is a path on four nodes whose consecutive facet pairs carry label infinity and whose non-consecutive (perpendicular) facet pairs carry label 2 (commuting). Because the orthoscheme is a Euclidean (affine) reflection group whose facet pairs do not all meet at angles that are rational multiples of Pi, a(n) is NOT known to coincide with the rational Coxeter (Steinberg-Solomon) Poincare word-growth series of any single fixed Coxeter system; no proven exact rational growth series is available for this sequence. The realized affine growth lies strictly below the free-Coxeter maximum, since the perpendicular facet pairs commute (m = 2).

Values computed by exact-rational BFS on legs (a,b,c) = (1,1,2) through depth 18 (terms a(0)..a(18)), triple-verified.

Leg-independence within Class B is empirically observed, not proven: identical initial terms were obtained by independent BFS on legs (1,1,2), (1,1,3), (2,2,3), (1,1,5) and (3,3,7). Dependence only on the equal/distinct symmetry pattern of the legs is conjectural in this class. (Leg-independence is proven only for Class C, the pairwise-distinct case; the proof there does not transfer, since it relies on all three dihedral angles being irrational multiples of Pi, which fails when two legs are equal.)

Empirically a(n+1)/a(n) is near 1.925; a(18)/a(17) = 1.9238. No minimal polynomial for the growth rate is known, since no rational generating function is known for this class.

Finite-range coincidence: the tetranacci recurrence t(n) = t(n-1) + t(n-2) + t(n-3) + t(n-4) (the recurrence underlying A251704) reproduces a(6) through a(13) but then diverges: it predicts 24615 at n = 14 whereas the true value is a(14) = 24591. The tetranacci dominant root 1.92756... is therefore a coincidence and is not the true growth rate (the empirical ratio trends below it).

Direct linear-algebra tests on the depth-18 data rule out a linear recurrence over Q of order <= 9.
