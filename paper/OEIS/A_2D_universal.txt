%N  The universal right-triangle reflection sequence: layer sizes a(n) of the BFS orbit of any right triangle in the plane with positive unequal legs under reflection across its three sides; the sequence is independent of the triangle (Theorem of Universality, Bonfioli 2026).

%C  Comments:
    Place a right triangle T in Q^2 with vertices V_0, V_1, V_2 and the
    right angle at V_2; let R_0, R_1, R_2 be reflections across the three
    sides. Let W = <R_0, R_1, R_2> with R_i^2 = 1 and (R_leg1 R_leg2)^2
    = 1 (the abstract Coxeter group of the right-angle pair). Then a(n)
    is the number of distinct affine matrices reached at minimal
    word-length n by BFS in the Cayley graph of W under the
    side-reflection realization rho_T.

    THEOREM OF UNIVERSALITY (Bonfioli 2026).  For every right triangle T
    with positive legs, ker(rho_T) is the same subgroup of W, and hence
    a(n) is independent of T for ALL n >= 0.  The theorem has two
    independent proofs:

      - Structural proof.  Mayer-Vietoris and Crowell exact sequences
        identify the abelianized translation lattice
        T_rho^{ab} ~= I_A intersect I_B as a left Z[Q]-module, where
        Q = D_infty x Z/2 is the linear quotient.  The intersection is
        the cyclic left ideal Z[Q] . h on the explicit central generator
        h = (c-1)(Y-1), with annihilator (Y+1) and (c+1) (exact
        identities in Z[Q]).  Cyclicity rests on the centrality of
        c = X X' in Q, which corresponds to the half-turn around the
        right-angle vertex.  Hence T_rho^{ab} ~= Z[Q]/(Y+1, c+1) =: M,
        and every rho_T factors through a single abstract realization
        rho_abs : W -> Q ltimes M (Bonfioli 2026, Lemma in §5).

      - Verification via Zariski-density.  For each canonical word
        w in W, the translation t(rho_{a,b}(w)) is a polynomial-rational
        function of (a, b).  By Zariski density, "t(w) = 0" either
        holds identically in (a, b) or fails on a Zariski-dense open
        set.  Verification at three Pythagorean specializations
        (a, b) in {(3,4), (5,12), (8,15)} for every canonical word
        w in W of length <= 18 (393,216 words) confirms the polynomial
        condition is uniform across (a, b): w in ker(rho_T) for one T
        iff for every T (Bonfioli 2026, independent verification).

    The asymptotic ratio beta_2 ~= 1.4718 is transcendental over Q
    (Theorem of Bonfioli 2026), established via three independent
    conditional routes, each resting on a different finite-horizon
    hypothesis that is empirically verified and structurally
    attackable:

      (i) Christol route. Conditional on Myhill-Nerode separation of
          the explicit family {((XY)^n, t_n) : n >= 1} inside the
          universal realisation rho_abs : W -> Q ltimes M. The
          underlying mod-p state-space infinity is UNCONDITIONAL for
          every prime p via the witness-monomial (XY)^{n-1} X . h of
          length 2n-1 in the alternating D_inf presentation. Empirical
          BFS data at p in {3, 5, 7}, depths {42, 40, 40}, gives the
          stronger exponential growth rates {1.4681, 1.5048, 1.5077}.
          Uses Christol 1979, Adamczewski-Bell 2017, Allouche-Shallit
          Theorem 6.6.2, Flajolet-Sedgewick VII.7.

      (ii) D-finite route. Conditional on extending the no-D-finite-
          recurrence verification from (k <= 9, m <= 7) to all finite
          (k, m). Uses Stanley EC2 Theorem 6.4.6 (algebraic =>
          D-finite) and Flajolet-Sedgewick VII.7.

      (iii) Mahler-structure route. Conditional on Adamczewski-Faverjon
          (Compositio Math. 156, 2020) style exclusion of class-(1)
          Mahler-ness for U(t).

    The three conditional hypotheses are logically independent; for
    transcendence of beta_2 to fail, all three would have to fail
    simultaneously.

    UNIVERSALITY CLASS (Bonfioli 2026, Theorem of Universality Class).
    The universality phenomenon extends beyond the right-triangle case
    to a class of free products W = A * B of finite groups with a
    linear quotient pi : W ->> Q satisfying explicit cyclic-ideal
    hypotheses on a central generator h in Z[Q].  Two instances are
    currently known: the right-triangle reflection group (W = V_4 *
    Z/2, this sequence), and the hyperbolic quadrilateral reflection
    group with two right angles at opposite vertices and two ideal
    vertices (W' = V_4 * V_4, cyclic generator h' = c - 1 with
    annihilator (c+1)).  The class theorem rules out acute/obtuse
    triangle groups (no central involution), triangle groups with all
    finite Coxeter labels (no free-product structure), the rectangle
    group D_inf x D_inf (direct product, not free product), and
    right-corner orthoschemes in dimension n >= 3 (cross-relations
    destroy the free-product structure); universality in this sense
    is specific to dimension 2.

    Geometric and combinatorial structure:
      - For 1 <= n <= 9: a(n) = F(n+3) (Fibonacci A000045), from
        Steinberg's word-growth formula applied to W with Poincare
        series (1+t)^2 / (1-t-t^2).
      - For n = 10: a(10) = 225 = F(13) - 8. The deficit of 8 is
        accounted for by exactly eight explicit length-10 affine
        relations in W, proven symbolically in Q(a, b) for generic
        legs (a, b) (Bonfioli 2026, Theorem 1 + completeness).
      - For n >= 11: a(n) is the common value of |{rho_T(w) : |w| = n}|
        for any right triangle T.

    The values shown are computed for the (3,4,5) triangle to depth 38
    and have been independently reproduced on (5,12,13), (7,24,25), and
    (1,2,sqrt(5)) through depth 22; on nine Pythagorean triples through
    depth 25.

    Empirically a(n+1)/a(n) approaches a limit near 1.4718 from above
    (paper §6); the sequence satisfies no linear recurrence of order
    <= 19 over Q (Berlekamp-Massey on depth-42 data), and independent
    tests rule out algebraic and holonomic generating function up to
    (Y-deg 4, x-deg 18) and (order 9, poly-deg 4) respectively. The
    exact algebraic nature of the asymptotic ratio remains open.

    For the isosceles right triangle (a = b), the position-count
    sequence |O_d(T)| under the orbit action coincides with a(n) only
    through depth 3 and is smaller thereafter (1, 3, 5, 8, 11, 13, 16,
    ...); the divergence reflects an additional element of the
    stabilizer of T inside rho_T(W) (the leg-swap symmetry).

%F  Formula:
    For 1 <= n <= 9: a(n) = F(n+3) where F = A000045.

    For n >= 10: a(n) <= [t^n] (1+t)^2/(1-t-t^2), strict.

    The generating function is NOT rational of degree <= 19, NOT
    algebraic up to (Y-deg 4, x-deg 18), NOT holonomic up to (order 9,
    poly-deg 4).
